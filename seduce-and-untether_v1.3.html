Seduce & Untether

where desire is strategy
"The most dangerous game is the one played with the heart."
What is Seduce & Untether?

A social strategy card game of influence, power, and entanglement for 2–5 players. Players use Seduction cards to place Tether tokens on each other — gaining Influence — while Untether and Special cards help break free, expose secrets, or reshape the board. The game rewards reading people more than playing optimally.

Setup

Choose your game length: ⚡ The Quickie (4 rounds), 🌹 The Conquest (6), 🔥 The Obsession (8), or 🌙 The Long Game (10).

Enter player names and emojis. Each player is secretly assigned a Vulnerability Trait — shown only on your turn in the sidebar.

Each player starts with a hand of 4 cards. One card is played per turn. After playing, one new card is drawn.

The device is passed between players. Each turn is private.

Turn Structure

Play — select a card from your hand, choose a target if required, and tap Play.

Resolve — the card effect applies immediately (Slow Burn activates next round).

Status Events — dramatic room-wide overlays may fire after certain seductions (That's Bold, Who Is That?).

Pass — hand the device to the next player via the handoff screen.

Tether Tokens

Tethers are placed on you by other players and represent entanglement. They're colour-coded by who placed them — always public. Reaching 10 total Tethers means you are Hopelessly Entangled and cannot win.

The Voyeur Gallery

Any player can place a secret wager between turns — tap Place a Secret Wager in the sidebar. Bet on whether a target will gain tethers, lose tethers, stay unscathed, or has a secret affair. Correct bets double your stake. Wrong bets cost it.

Secret Affairs

Tap Secret Affair in the sidebar to propose one privately. If accepted, both players gain +2 Influence. Affairs are searchable by Caught You and Walk of Shame — unless protected by Plausible Deniability. Lover's Night Out rendezvous are stored separately and invisible to all accusation cards.

Status Events

That's Bold — fires at 40% when the top scorer seduces someone. All watchers gain +1 Tether toward the leader. Leader gains +1 Influence.

Who Is That? — fires at 40% when the lowest scorer tethers the top scorer. All watchers gain +1 Tether toward the underdog. Underdog gains +2 Influence.

Why Seduction Can Fail

These three passive rules introduce friction, misjudgement, and social pressure — punishing players who treat seduction as a guaranteed transaction.

😬 Overreach Penalty

Trigger: You play a Seduction on someone who has more Influence than you AND has zero Tether tokens pointing toward you.

Effect: The seduction fails entirely. Card discarded, no effect. You still draw a replacement.

You must earn the right to reach for someone out of your orbit. Build connection first, or close the Influence gap.

💔 Thread Snap

Trigger: You play a Seduction on someone who already has 3 or more Tether tokens from you specifically.

Effect: Instead of adding a Tether, one is removed. The thread recoils.

Too much pressure past saturation causes retreat. Diversify your seductions — obsessing over one target will backfire.

👥 Audience Effect

Trigger: You play a Seduction from Round 3 onward with 2 or more other players watching.

Effect: Regardless of outcome, you lose 1 Influence immediately after resolution.

Every seduction under observation carries a social cost. In a 2-player game this rule is inactive.

How They Interact

The Audience Effect punishes aggression. The Overreach Penalty punishes reaching too far. The Thread Snap punishes fixation. Together they reward reading the room over brute-force card play — and keep the web from collapsing into a two-player dynamic.

End Conditions

The game ends when the chosen number of rounds completes, or earlier if a player hits 10 Tethers. There are two ways to win.

🏆 Influence Champion
After all rounds complete, the player with the most Influence wins. Seduced widely, accumulated power, stayed free enough to claim victory.
✨ The Untethered
Reach 0 Tethers AND 10+ Influence at any point — instant win. Full autonomy without sacrificing power. The rarest and most elegant victory.
💔 Hopelessly Entangled
Reaching 10 or more total Tethers ends the game immediately. The remaining player with the most Influence wins instead.
✦ Strategy Tips

The Fade — most underused card. Removes your Tethers, gains you Influence, and pulls the target closer all at once.

Love Bomb — massive swing but you catch feelings. Best when you can absorb the self-tether.

The Nightcap — invisible on the board. Nobody will suspect you did anything.

Plausible Deniability — holding it is psychological warfare. Make them afraid to accuse you.

Smoke Screen — looks identical to a full Shield publicly. Use the ambiguity.

Midnight Confession — reveals traits mutually. Only use if you're willing to be known.

Persona Swap — most lethal when someone is sitting close to 10 Tethers.

Lover's Night Out — if accepted, the affair is completely invisible to Caught You and Walk of Shame.
